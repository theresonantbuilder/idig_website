# We The Resonant Builders
### A Manifesto for the Observer Resonance Movement
*by J. Paul Duplantis*

---

## The Argument

The next architecture of human communication will not be built on attention capture. It will be built on **observer resonance**. Hilbert Space is not just a mathematical framework — it is the geometry we have always needed to model how meaning actually works between people.

This book is not about iDIG. iDIG is one proof of concept. This book is an argument — and an invitation — to builders across every domain: healthcare, education, talent, social infrastructure, media, community. The framework is universal. The movement is just beginning.

---

## The Companion Projects

This book is being written in parallel with three interconnected builds:

- **iDIG — Nonprofit Open Protocol** — transitioning from product to shared neutral layer
- **Worker Cooperative** — the governance model for the protocol's stewardship
- **Crowdfunding Campaign** — funding the nonprofit transition and protocol infrastructure

The essays and the builds inform each other. The book is the public record of all three.

## Word Count & Page Estimate

*Based on The Awakening at ~1,350 words (essay body, frontmatter excluded). Assessed April 2026.*

### At current web essay length
| Component | Words |
|-----------|-------|
| 20 essays × 1,350 | 27,000 |
| 5 interstitials × 400 | 2,000 |
| Preface + front/back matter | ~1,000 |
| **Total** | **~30,000 words / ~120 pages** |

This is web length — solid for the weekly series but short for a standalone book.

### Target print length (recommended)
Expand each essay to 2,000–2,500 words during the edit pass after all 20 are published. The expansion happens in revision, not in the weekly writing.

| Component | Words |
|-----------|-------|
| 20 essays × 2,200 avg | 44,000 |
| 5 interstitials × 600 | 3,000 |
| Preface + front/back matter | ~2,000 |
| **Total** | **~49,000 words / ~195 pages** |

**195 pages is the target** — purposeful and tight, consistent with comp titles in this genre (*The Cathedral and the Bazaar*, Seth Godin's manifestos). Not padded, not thin.

> The weekly essays are the first draft. The edit pass is where web length becomes book length.
