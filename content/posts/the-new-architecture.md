In the exploration of the iDIG Protocol, I've often focused on the technical mechanics of resonance. But as I look at the friction inherent in the modern labor market, it becomes clear that we aren't just looking for a new tool — we are looking for a **new grammar of interaction**.

HiringSignals.ai is not intended to be the final word on how we hire. Rather, it is an experiment — a living laboratory designed to explore how open-source protocols can more effectively align human potential with opportunity. By treating iDIG as an experimental probe, we can begin to see the outlines of a future where talent is no longer a "resource" to be siloed, but a signal to be discovered.

## The 2-Terabyte Reality

To understand the opportunity, we must look at the physical scale of the talent data problem. If we were to take the resumes of every active professional in the United States, parse them into clean, structured data, and store them efficiently, the entire collection would occupy less than **2 terabytes of storage**.

> The collective professional potential of an entire nation can fit on a single, consumer-grade external drive.

This reveals a profound truth: the challenge of modern hiring isn't a "Big Data" problem. It is an **access and alignment problem**. When human potential is fragmented across proprietary silos, the signal begins to degrade. The friction we experience today is an architectural limitation of "walled gardens" that must prioritize platform-specific engagement over universal resonance.

## iDIG as an Experimental Probe: The Vector Anatomy

In this experiment, we aren't just matching keywords. We are testing how we might map resonance across three primary dimensions of the professional experience:

- **The Social Vector** — The network of trust and community resonance.
- **The Semantic Vector** — The explicit language of skills and conceptual frameworks.
- **The Experiential Vector** — The temporal mechanics of a professional journey.

By breaking the talent signal into these components, HiringSignals.ai acts as a test case for higher-fidelity alignment. We are asking: can we create a discovery engine that respects the complexity of the human Observer?

## Enriched Breadcrumbs: Reimagining the Language of Capability

Within this vector space, we introduce the concept of **Enriched Breadcrumbs**. Traditional resumes and job descriptions are "lossy" formats — they strip away the nuance of intent and the reality of day-to-day mechanics. By utilizing Large Language Models to enrich the vectors from both the candidate (the Observer) and the hiring manager, we can generate a trail of breadcrumbs that capture the "unspoken" requirements of a role.

These LLM-enriched breadcrumbs allow for a reimagining of capability:

- **Dynamic Stack Ranking** — Skills are no longer binary checkboxes but are stack-ranked based on the frequency and depth of the breadcrumbs left behind by past projects and real-time status updates.
- **Discovery of Latent Capabilities** — We can surface capabilities that wouldn't necessarily reside within a static resume — such as a specific problem-solving methodology or a unique "vibe" in team collaboration — by analyzing the resonance between a candidate's enriched narrative and the hiring manager's actual intent.
- **Contextual Alignment** — The breadcrumbs allow the protocol to understand that a candidate's experience in one domain has high-dimensional alignment with a seemingly unrelated challenge in another, moving us past the limitations of traditional keyword matching.

## Preserving the Superposition: Avoiding the Early Collapse

One of the core hypotheses of this experiment is the need to protect the Observer's agency. It is critical to note that the "Observer" in this ecosystem is not just the candidate — it is also the hiring manager. Both parties inhabit this Hilbert Space, looking for mutual resonance.

In the current ecosystem, the moment a candidate signals "availability," they experience a **premature collapse of the wave function**. Their privacy is compromised, their current professional standing is at risk, and the market observes them before they are ready. This fear creates massive friction, keeping the most talented individuals from ever entering the field of discovery.

The iDIG experiment proposes a model of **Real-Time State Discovery with private permissions**:

- **Latent Availability** — A candidate can update their status to "Active" within the protocol. Their resonance becomes discoverable to companies seeking their specific vector, but their identity remains in a state of superposition.
- **Sovereign Disclosure** — The identity is only revealed when the candidate — the Observer — chooses to collapse the wave function after seeing a resonant signal from a potential partner.

> This isn't just a "feature" — it's a proposal for how all talent protocols might eventually handle the delicate balance of visibility and privacy.

## Toward a Multi-Protocol Future: Dominion and the W3C

The iDIG Protocol is designed to work in harmony with existing open standards, rather than in competition. We lean into the W3C standards — specifically the **Solid Pod protocol** — to ensure that both candidates and hiring managers maintain absolute dominion over their own content.

In this architecture, data is never shared or modified without explicit permission. By housing encrypted information in private data stores or directly on the user's own device via IndexedDB storage, we ensure that professional identity remains sovereign. The protocol acts as the matchmaker in the Hilbert Space, but the data itself resides under the total control of the individual.

We are in the early days of a transition from **platform ownership to personal orchestration**. Whether the future is built on iDIG or other emerging decentralized standards, the goal remains the same: to return agency to the individual.

HiringSignals.ai is our contribution to that dialogue — an attempt to treat talent alignment as a matter of quantum resonance, ensuring that the 2 terabytes of our collective ambition are no longer locked in silos, but are free to find the opportunities that truly resonate with who we are and what we can become.
