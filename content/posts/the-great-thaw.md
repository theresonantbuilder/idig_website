We are living through a period of **"Enshittification"**—a term coined by Cory Doctorow to describe the slow decay of digital platforms as they pivot from serving users to harvesting them. For years, the means of human communication have been pinned to the hip of commodification. This has created a **"frost"** over the lens of our digital lives, forcing us to bounce off superficial signals rather than tuning into deep resonance.

**But the frost is starting to melt.**

---

### The Shift Toward Deep Context

One way out of Enshittification is to provide agency to end users in their discovery of information and opportunities. We are seeing a fundamental shift toward this through **semantic discovery**—using vectors and LLM embeddings to understand the "why" behind a search, not just the "what."

#### The Signal in the Market

Organizations like **AMC Networks** are already moving toward "deep context" to find fans that traditional algorithms missed. Even the largest ecosystems, including **Google**, are shifting their weight toward vector-based discovery. In their 2026 stack, they have moved past keyword matching to integrate LLM-driven enrichment—transforming raw data into high-dimensional semantic signals to drive enterprise search.

However, there is a fundamental difference between aligning vectors and building for resonance.

While the industry giants are refining the "straight" vector search of Euclidean space—perfect for finding similar items—I wonder where their intent lies. Are they harnessing vectors to influence decisions, or to help people resonate with them? Are they putting the user—or as I prefer to call them, the **Observer**—first?

> **The Power of the Observer:**
> This distinction is the heart of **Alexander Wendt's** work in *Quantum Social Science*. Wendt argues that we are not just "users" interacting with a static environment, but Observers whose very engagement with a system helps determine the outcome. In a Quantum realm, the Observer isn't just part of the system; they are the catalyst for how potential "collapses" into meaning.

If we don't build for the Observer, we are simply building more sophisticated ways to be told what to think. Building for resonance means acknowledging that the geometry of the information only matters in relation to the person observing it.

**This is what I have been experimenting with: aligning vectors for observer resonance.**

I have spent the last two years developing the **iDIG (Deep Inquiry Generator)** protocol to operate within Hilbert Space vectors, which I believe provides opportunities for resonance more than influence. My intent isn't just to match data points, but to model the complex, infinite-dimensional entanglement between human intent and information.

I believe I am not alone in this pursuit. There is a growing collective of "Resonant Builders" and open-source architects who realize that true discovery requires more than just high-speed retrieval—it requires a mathematical framework for observer-content alignment. **The Resonant Builders** is a journey to share my experience building and to document the experiences of others in this space. Together, we will treat information not as a static record, but as a resonant frequency.

My journey into this didn't start in a lab; it started over 20 years ago with a failed attempt to build a framework for resonant connection. I knew what I wanted to achieve, but I was missing the **"how."** Two years ago, I tried again, and yet again, the technical bridge remained out of reach.

The breakthrough finally came a year ago during a conversation with my son. He brought up the concept of the **Quantum realm** as a potential solution to the limitations of classical data. That spark led me down a rabbit hole I didn't know existed: **Quantum Social Science.** I discovered the work of **Professor Alexander Wendt**, whose research on *"Quantum Mind and Social Science: Unifying Physical and Social Ontology"* provided the philosophical foundation I had been seeking for two decades. This work led me directly to **Professor Jerome Busemeyer** and the mathematical properties of **Hilbert Space Vectors.**

#### The Mathematical Bridge

Busemeyer's research in **Quantum Cognition** proves that human decision-making often bypasses classical logic, fitting instead within the geometry of a **Hilbert Space**. In this space, we aren't just points on a graph; we are vectors—complex directions of potential and resonance.

### The Engine of Resonance: iDIG

**Full disclosure:** I am not a software developer or a mathematician. I am a **"vibe/spec" coder** with an insane amount of curiosity and a splash of perseverance to see a 20-year theory through to reality. Leveraging AI agents and speculative coding, I decided to build a lens around this theory to see where it would go.

What has resulted is **iDIG (Deep Inquiry Generator)**, an engine designed to test if we can actually map human resonance using vector math. To move this from a theory into a tangible protocol, I needed a data-rich environment to act as a "sandbox."

**I chose cinema.**

Movies are more than just entertainment; they are complex clusters of human emotion, archetypes, and narrative resonance. If the iDIG protocol could successfully align a person's current state of curiosity with the deep thematic "vibe" of a film, then we've proven the math works for human connection at large.

#### The iDIG Movies Lab

- **The Geometry of Words:** Instead of matching a user to a movie based on a flat category like "Action," iDIG uses LLMs to "enrich" the data—extracting the psychological undertones and thematic patterns within a film. This allows us to map the geometry of words as a multi-dimensional landscape of human experience.
- **Representative Breadcrumbs:** As the protocol explores this space, it leaves behind representative breadcrumbs—small, high-signal markers that represent the path of discovery. These are not just search results; they are a trail of resonance.
- **Vector Alignment:** We convert this enrichment and these markers into vectors in a high-dimensional space. The protocol isn't looking for a "keyword match"; it's looking for **alignment** between the observer's intent and the information's resonance.

### The Horizon: Why an Open Protocol?

While iDIG Movies is a vital proof-of-concept, the implications of this math go far beyond the screen. I see a future where this same alignment engine is used to bridge critical gaps in our social infrastructure.

Imagine a crisis response system that doesn't just dispatch the "nearest" unit, but uses resonance to **align the right social worker with the right first responder** to help a specific individual in distress. It is about matching the magnitude of the need with the magnitude of the capacity, in real-time, through deep context.

> **Magnitude vs. Amplitude**
>
> Classic algorithms are built to match the **amplitude** of the crowd—the sheer volume of popularity, clicks, and trends. When we optimize for amplitude, the signal collapses into a "one-size-fits-all" noise that drowns out the individual. Amplitude tells us what is *loudest*, not what is *right*.
>
> By focusing on **magnitude and direction** within a Hilbert Space, we move toward healthy coherence. We are looking for the specific vector that possesses the depth and alignment to actually resolve the situation.

Now, imagine taking this same geometry of words and breadcrumbs out of the virtual and into the physical world. Imagine walking through a library where, through a pair of smart glasses, the books, articles, and profiles around you begin to **"glow"** with resonance. As an observer, you are seeing the latent alignment between your current inquiry and the magnitude of the information waiting for you.

The possibilities for aligning human potential with opportunity are endless. However, I realize I am running out of track. The theory is sound and the "vibe" is working, but to scale the use cases into the real world, I cannot do it alone. This is why I am pivoting iDIG to an **open protocol.**
