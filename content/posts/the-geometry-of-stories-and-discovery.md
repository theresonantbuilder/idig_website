iDIG Movies is a film discovery experience built on the idea that the right film finds you — not through what's popular or trending, but through what resonates with who you are and how you're feeling right now.

Every film in this collection has been scored against a taxonomy of thematic trails — emotional, social, philosophical, and spiritual dimensions that make a film matter to a specific person on a specific night. When you choose a genre, a trail, or a mood, the grid reorders instantly based on pre-computed resonance scores. No algorithms watching your behavior. No collaborative filtering. Just the intersection of a curated taxonomy and your own intent.

The architecture is inspired by Hilbert spaces found in Quantum theory — not as a formula, but as a philosophy. You exist in a state of many possible resonances simultaneously. The films that surface are the ones that can hold that complexity without collapsing it into a single label.

Each trail lives as a vector within a pillar — Wonder, Social, Experiential, Semantic — and every film holds a position across all of them. When you select a trail, you aren't filtering. You're rotating the space, amplifying certain dimensions and letting others recede, so that what rises to the surface is the geometry of your intent reflected back through the catalog.

## Story Pathways

Every film opens a door to something beyond itself — a book that shaped its DNA, a soundtrack that carries its emotional weight, a place that breathes its atmosphere, a conversation around characters, or clothing styles that inspire a certain emotion.

Story Pathways are curated artifacts connected to each film through the same thematic trails that brought the film to you.

This is an experiment in resonance-driven discovery. Instead of targeting ads at demographic profiles or browsing behavior, Story Pathways tune recommendations to signals that live deep within the content itself. A film scored high on "Sacred Spaces" surfaces travel to the locations that inspired it. A film resonating with "Laughing Through Tears" connects to books that carry that same emotional signature. The trail that found the film also finds the artifact.

No tracking cookies. No purchase history. No behavioral surveillance. The signal comes from the content, not the consumer — and the observer chooses which trails to walk.

## Observer Agency

In a Hilbert space, the observer is not a data point — the observer is the measurement itself. iDIG Movies honors this by putting every interaction in your hands. You choose the trails. You choose the mood. You rate the resonance. Nothing watches you in the background, nothing nudges you toward a purchase, and nothing pops up to interrupt your exploration.

The space responds to your intent, not your history. This is discovery without surveillance. A field you move through, not a funnel you're moved through.

## Community Protocol

iDIG Movies is not a streaming platform. It's a protocol for building film libraries — each one curated for a specific group, scored against its own thematic landscape, and connected to the world around it.

A film club in New Orleans curates 200 films. An independent filmmaker collective in Portland builds a library around their members' work and influences. A university course on post-colonial cinema assembles 50 films scored against trails the professor designed. Each library is its own Hilbert space — same architecture, completely different dimensions.

The advertising model follows the same principle. When a film scores high on "Sacred Spaces" and a local bookstore two blocks from the film club specializes in architecture and spirituality, that's not a coincidence an algorithm manufactured — it's a genuine thematic alignment between content and commerce. A neighborhood restaurant whose menu echoes the culinary traditions in a film. A local travel agency that specializes in the regions a film brings to life. A vintage clothing shop whose inventory resonates with a film's aesthetic period.

This is what advertising looks like when the signal comes from content instead of surveillance. Even if the ads come from a large corporate entity, how can they resonate with the observer and the stories they are related to?

The vision is a network of these spaces — each one small enough to be personal, connected enough to be powerful, and open enough that anyone with a list of films and a community to share them with can build one.

## Moving Forward

iDIG Movies is the opening salvo. Movies is actually a subset of what I'm calling Stories — a broader protocol that will cover films, episodics, books, and comic books, all scored against the same thematic trail architecture.

I also have Hiring Signals in active development, applying the same resonance model to align talent with opportunity, and Mental Health Pathways in the conceptual stage.

But the immediate goal is simpler: test iDIG Movies with real observers and gather honest feedback on what's working and what isn't. I'll be the first to admit the interface can feel overwhelming — but in the spirit of Hilbert spaces, easy access is not always healthy access. The goal is to surface what deeply resonates with the discoverer, and what resonates with us is rarely what's easiest to find.

That said, my vision for discovery is almost certainly not yours. I hand-curated every trail in this interface by breaking down film catalogs across multiple LLM models. Should my trails feed your discovery? Probably not. But I also don't see most people spending their time building custom trail taxonomies from scratch. So the plan is to add the ability to create custom trails and eventually allow observers within the protocol to collaborate on shared taxonomies — communities shaping their own dimensions of meaning.

On curation more broadly, I see real opportunity for collaborators to build and maintain their own community libraries and to share in the advertising revenue those communities generate. The protocol is designed to make that possible.

There are endless directions iDIG Movies could go. But the path I will fight to stay away from — with everything I have — is the one paved in gold. The moment iDIG is primarily driven by revenue is the moment the protocol collapses in on itself. I can theorize about Hilbert spaces all I want, but if the motivating factor behind a communication protocol is rooted in maximizing profit, quality in that communication cannot survive. To preserve profitability, decisions eventually get made that sacrifice the health of the connection. That's not magical thinking. It's just reality.

An example of the tension I have with revenue driving the build is an idea I have for reverse engineering the ad spaces against a catalogue of movies where I build a movie collection around a major retailer's offerings. The only way I could justify generating revenue from this is if I featured small business owner's offerings as well to help satisfy the health of the engagement. It is the intent I carry with what I have built on my own from scratch that I believe helps create the healthy space people will engage with.

And this is where I wrestle with open-sourcing the protocol. I have plans to seek collaborators to build off the protocol in the future but I don't want just anyone building off of it. If the primary motivation is to make money, there are far better ways to spend their time. But if there is a desire to help inject health into the connection between people and things that move them — they'd be invited to collaborate and build. To help shape the space.

iDIG Movies is in active testing — this is not a stable release. Things will break, layouts will shift, and features will appear and disappear as I iterate. That's by design. I'd rather build in the open and learn from real observers than polish in isolation.

One of the most valuable things you can do right now is give feedback on the trails themselves. The thematic dimensions you see in the Attract panel — trails like "Laughing Through Tears" or "Crisis of Faith" or "The Trick Is on You" — are the building blocks of the Hilbert space. When you select a trail combination and the results feel right, that's the space working. When they feel off, that's a signal that a trail needs to be retuned, redefined, or replaced. Your perspective on whether these dimensions actually capture something meaningful is what will increase the quality of the discovery space over time.

The resonance feedback built into the interface — the Weak, Close, and Strong indicators next to your active trails — feeds directly into this tuning process. Every response helps calibrate the alignment between what the trails promise and what the films deliver.
